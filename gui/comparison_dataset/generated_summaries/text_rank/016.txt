Born in Luton , Bedfordshire , on 5 April 1920 , Hailey was the only child of working class parents , They could not afford to keep him in school beyond the age of 14 . The thriller follows events in the sky , and on the ground at a snow-logged airport , when a terrorist boards an airplane with a bomb . In 1969 , he moved to Nassau in the Bahamas , with his wife Sheila . Despite a lukewarm response from critics , and few literary accolades , Hailey was at the height of his fame in the seventies and continued to attract the attention of Hollywood producers . Strong Medicine , Hailey 's blockbuster focusing on the pharmaceutical industry , was turned into a film in 1986 , starring Sam Neill , Douglas Fairbanks Jr and a panoply of former soap stars .
