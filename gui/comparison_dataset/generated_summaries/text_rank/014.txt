Paul Stewart and Chris Riddell 's book came top in the category for six- to eight-year-olds and won the award chosen by after-school club members . The prize , which is celebrating its 20th year , is billed as `` the UK 's biggest children 's book award '' . As well as the six to eights prize , it won the 4Children Special Award voted for by after-school club members . Julia Eccleshare , chair of the adult judging panel , said children 's literature had `` never looked stronger '' in the prize 's 20 years . `` This award counts because the final choice of winners is made by children , who are the toughest critics of all , '' she said .
